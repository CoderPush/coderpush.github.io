Who we are#

CoderPush is a studio of engineers and designers who collaborate with clients for great software, teams, and processes.

We set a very high expectation on client satisfaction for the long term. See reviews on Clutch.

What you’ll work on#

We want to be coworkers for years to come, and you need to learn new tech. However, in the short term, here are things you can do:

  • Help make Rails apps with high traffic to be more performant on AWS. Be familiar with setting up ElastiCache (Redis, Memcached), ElasticSearch, load balancing (ECS, RDS) and so on.
  • Contribute to maintaining our self-hosted open-source applications, such as Mattermost (Slack alternative).
  • Learn, practice, taking exam and become an AWS Certified DevOps Engineer Professional!

PS: too easy? We’ve got teams using other backends (e.g. Python, Node, Elixir) as well as frontend (mostly React ecosystem).

PS2: Why AWS you may ask? While we can (and did) work with projects on AWS, GCP and others, we decided to go all-in on AWS. Because you know, one can do anything but not everything! We prefer to be excellent in one focused expertise rather than being average on everything!

We are looking for someone with#

  • 2 years of experiences. Familiar with AWS is a plus!
  • Good communication skills in English and Vietnamese.
  • Humility to learn and share what you don’t know, everyday.
  • Ability to understand and define all actions to be performed when assigned to a task.
  • Ability to manage time when facing difficulties, under pressure or deadlines.
  • Codemunication skills. For example, as a DevOps, it means loving “infrastructure as code” and “diagram as code”.

Curiosity and patience in DevOpSec practice. For example, some will find this summary shared by our coworker boring, but you love it and want to add more to it.

Personal growth#

Still here? You must be thinking about how you’ll grow. That’s one of our core values.

Why join a young company instead of a big one? Because you can learn faster, make mistakes, share learning from mistakes, and have personal impact on those around you. You shape how the company forms.

Why a software studio instead of a product company? You do not get stuck in a product or a product team. You get coaching and mentorship not from only your project team but from other project teams as well. We build to last, without the pressure of investment capital.

Why CoderPush instead of just another “devshop”? Because we build teams first, products second. We have been cofounders of a bilingual school, a coding school, a running club, and a few funded startups (let’s exchange stories!).

And lastly, join to build a company like building great code itself: continuous improvement, continuous deployment, refactoring, technical debt, and contributing to open source.
